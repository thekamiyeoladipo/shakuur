Building Resilience in Kids: Teaching Problem-Solving and Coping Skills

Posted by Olaoluwa Adbulshakuur on February, 2019

Children face numerous challenges every day, from navigating friendships and managing schoolwork to dealing with family changes or even more serious issues like bullying. Without the ability to solve problems or cope with stress, these challenges can become overwhelming. A child who lacks problem-solving and coping skills may feel stuck, unsure of how to proceed when faced with a difficult situation. This can lead to frustration, anxiety, or even avoidance behavior, where the child simply gives up when something seems too challenging. This ability, known as resilience, is not an innate trait but a skill that can be nurtured and developed. Two fundamental components of resilience are problem-solving and coping skills. Together, these skills form the backbone of resilience, empowering children to handle life's inevitable ups and downs with confidence.
